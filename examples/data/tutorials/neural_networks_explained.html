Understanding Neural Networks

Neural networks are a set of algorithms, modeled loosely after the human brain, that are designed to recognize patterns. They interpret sensory data through a kind of machine perception, labeling or clustering raw input.

Structure of a Neural Network

Neural networks consist of the following components:

  • Input Layer: Receives the initial data.
  • Hidden Layers: Where the computations happen. Most neural networks have one or more hidden layers.
  • Output Layer: Produces the final result.
  • Weights and Biases: Parameters that are adjusted during training to optimize performance.
  • Activation Functions: Such as ReLU, Sigmoid, or Tanh, which introduce non-linearity into the network.

Types of Neural Networks

Feedforward Neural Networks

Information travels in one direction from input to output.

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

Specialized for processing grid-like data, such as images.

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)

Designed for sequential data, where context from previous inputs influences the processing of current input.

Transformers

A newer architecture that has revolutionized NLP and is now being applied to other domains as well.

Training a Neural Network

Neural networks learn through a process called backpropagation, which involves:

  1. Forward pass: Computing outputs given inputs.
  2. Computing the loss: Measuring how far the outputs are from the desired values.
  3. Backward pass: Calculating gradients of the loss with respect to the weights.
  4. Updating weights: Adjusting the weights to reduce the loss.

Advanced Concepts

More sophisticated neural network concepts include:

  • Transfer learning
  • Generative adversarial networks (GANs)
  • Attention mechanisms
  • Graph neural networks
